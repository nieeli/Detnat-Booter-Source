Lindworm in British heraldry, is a technical term for a wingless bipedal dragon often with a venomous bite, like a poisonous snake or Komodo dragon. Lindworms are very large and eat cattle and bodies, sometimes invading churchyards and eating the dead from cemeteries. The shed skin of a lindworm was believed to greatly increase a person's knowledge about nature and medicine.
